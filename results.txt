### Papers on Machine Learning:

1.  **Changing Data Sources in the Age of Machine Learning for Official Statistics** by Cedric De Boom, Michael Reusens (2023-06-07)
    *   **Summary:** This paper discusses the risks and challenges associated with changing data sources in machine learning for official statistics, covering technical, ethical, and regulatory aspects. It highlights repercussions like concept drift and bias, and suggests precautionary measures for maintaining integrity and reliability.
    *   **PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2306.04338v1

2.  **DOME: Recommendations for supervised machine learning validation in biology** by Ian Walsh, Dmytro Fishman, Dario Garcia-Gasulla, Tiina Titma, Gianluca Pollastri, The ELIXIR Machine Learning focus group, Jen Harrow, Fotis E. Psomopoulos, Silvio C. E. Tosatto (2020-06-25)
    *   **Summary:** This paper presents community-wide recommendations for supervised machine learning validation in biology, proposing a structured methods description (DOME: data, optimization, model, evaluation) to help assess performance and limitations.
    *   **PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2006.16189v4

3.  **Learning Curves for Decision Making in Supervised Machine Learning: A Survey** by Felix Mohr, Jan N. van Rijn (2022-01-28)
    *   **Summary:** This survey categorizes learning curve approaches in supervised machine learning, which are used for decision-making in contexts like data acquisition, early stopping, and model selection. It provides a framework for understanding different learning curve models.
    *   **PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2201.12150v2

### Papers on Generative AI:

1.  **Foundations of GenIR** by Qingyao Ai, Jingtao Zhan, Yiqun Liu (2025-01-06)
    *   **Summary:** This chapter explores the foundational impact of modern generative AI models on information access systems, focusing on information generation and synthesis, and discusses their applications and challenges.
    *   **PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2501.02842v1

2.  **AI prediction leads people to forgo guaranteed rewards** by Aoi Naito, Hirokazu Shirado (2026-03-30)
    *   **Summary:** This paper demonstrates how AI's predictive authority can influence human decision-making, leading individuals to forgo guaranteed rewards, even when predictions fail.
    *   **PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2603.28944v1

3.  **Competing Visions of Ethical AI: A Case Study of OpenAI** by Melissa Wilfley, Mengting Ai, Madelyn Rose Sanfilippo (2026-01-23)
    *   **Summary:** This case study analyzes OpenAI's public discourse on ethical AI, revealing that safety and risk discussions dominate, often without applying academic ethics frameworks.
    *   **PDF:** https://arxiv.org/pdf/2601.16513v1
